# Behavioral prep — STAR stories mapped to the role

Forward-deployed / field roles weight **customer-facing judgment** as heavily as coding. Prepare 6 stories in **STAR** (Situation, Task, Action, Result) and deliver each in under 2 minutes. Map them to what Fireworks is actually screening for.

## The 6 stories you must have ready

| # | Story theme | Proves (from the JD) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Shipped code in someone else's production** | "shipped code running in someone else's production environment" |
| 2 | **Navigated ambiguity / undefined problem** | discovery, "translate pain points into solutions" |
| 3 | **Executive / stakeholder communication** | "present to a VP", align stakeholders |
| 4 | **Debugged a hard production issue under pressure** | "debug a latency issue with an ML engineer" |
| 5 | **Conflict / pushback (customer or internal)** | trust, "credibility from what you build" |
| 6 | **Turned field signal into product change** | "feed customer signals back into the product roadmap" |

Bonus: a **failure** story (what broke, what you learned) and a **technical depth** story (a gnarly inference/fine-tuning win).

## STAR skeleton (fill these in for each)

```
Situation : the context + stakes (1 sentence)
Task      : what YOU specifically owned
Action    : the 2-3 concrete things you did (this is 60% of the story)
Result    : the measurable outcome + what you learned
```

Keep **Action** technical and specific; keep **Result** quantified ("cut p95 latency 3×", "shipped in 2 weeks", "became the default pattern for 4 accounts").

## FDE-specific behavioral questions (rehearse answers)

1. **"Tell me about a time you embedded with a customer's team and shipped something in their codebase."** → Story 1. Emphasize working *inside* their constraints, earning trust, leaving them better off.
2. **"Describe a customer engagement that started ambiguous. How did you find the real problem?"** → Story 2 + your discovery framework.
3. **"You're on-site, the demo is in 2 hours, and it's broken. What do you do?"** → Story 4. Triage calmly, communicate status, scope a fallback.
4. **"Tell me about disagreeing with a customer's technical direction."** → Story 5. You diagnosed, showed data, proposed the better path without ego; or you disagreed-and-committed.
5. **"How have you influenced a product roadmap from the field?"** → Story 6. Specific, urgent signal → concrete product change.
6. **"A VP asks why their inference bill is so high. Walk me through the conversation."** → discovery + the $/1M-token math (modules 08/09), honest trade-offs.
7. **"Tell me about a time you were wrong."** → failure story; ownership + the lesson + how you changed.
8. **"Why field engineering and not pure SWE / pure sales?"** → you want the **dual mandate**: build *and* own the relationship; "the quality of your engineering is the relationship."

## Why-Fireworks / why-this-role (have a crisp answer)

- You want to **build in production with frontier customers**, not just advise.
- Fireworks is at the **infrastructure frontier** (fastest inference, own function-calling/multimodal models, RL research from production systems).
- The **AI-native segment** means deep, fast, code-heavy engagements — your sweet spot.
- You're energized by **compressing the field→roadmap loop** and by on-site, in-person trust-building.

## Questions that reveal you understand the role (turn it around)

- "What does a great first 90 days look like for a Field Engineer here?"
- "How do field signals actually reach the product/eng roadmap today?"
- "What's the split between greenfield POCs vs scaling existing accounts in the AI-native segment?"
- "Where do engagements most often stall — integration, eval, or scaling — and how does the team unblock them?"

## Delivery tips

- **Lead with the result**, then back into how. Busy interviewers reward signal-first.
- **Quantify** everything you can.
- **"I" not "we"** for your actions; credit the team in the result.
- **Tie back to the role**: end a story with "…which is exactly the kind of embedded, ship-in-their-repo work this role is."
- **Show both gears**: at least one story where you debugged deep code *and* handled the exec conversation.
